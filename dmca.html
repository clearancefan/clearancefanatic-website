Filing a DMCA Takedown Notice

To file a DMCA takedown notice, provide our designated agent with the following:

  • A physical or electronic signature of a person authorized to act on behalf of the copyright owner
  • Identification of the copyrighted work you claim has been infringed
  • Identification of the infringing material on our site with enough detail to locate it (e.g., specific URL)
  • Your contact information including address, telephone number, and email address
  • A statement of good faith belief that the use is not authorized by the copyright owner, its agent, or the law
  • A statement, under penalty of perjury, that the information is accurate and you are authorized to act on the copyright owner's behalf

Our Response

Upon receipt of a valid DMCA notice, we will remove or disable access to the allegedly infringing content, notify the content provider where applicable, and follow the DMCA counter-notification process if one is received.

Counter-Notification

If you believe your content was removed in error, you may file a counter-notification including your signature, identification of the removed content, a statement under penalty of perjury that you have a good faith belief the content was removed by mistake, and your contact information and consent to jurisdiction of federal court in your district.
